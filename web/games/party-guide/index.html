Fun with Friends

Games to play with friends,
anywhere, anytime.

Eeee Oooo

One person leaves the room while the group chooses a specific task for them to perform (e.g., "shake hands with John" or "sit on the blue chair"). When they return, they must guess and perform the task. As they move around, the group guides them: if they get closer to the objective, everyone says "Eeee"; if they move away, everyone says "Oooo". The person wins when they successfully complete the hidden task.

The Psychologist

One player acts as the "Psychologist" and leaves the room. The group decides on a collective "condition" or "illness" they all share (e.g., "everyone thinks they are a cat" or "everyone repeats the last word of the person sitting next to them"). The Psychologist returns and must diagnose the condition by asking individual players questions. Players must answer truthfully according to their role.

Impostor

A game of deception and quick thinking. Everyone in the group is given a secret word or context, except for one or two players (the Impostors). Everyone must take turns saying one word related to the secret. Impostors must try to blend in without knowing the word. After a round, everyone votes on who they think is the Impostor.

The Dark Room

A high-stakes variation of hide-and-seek played in a completely dark environment. One person hides, and the rest search for them. Seekers are allowed to talk to each other to coordinate their search. When a seeker finds the hidden person, they must silently hide with them. The last seeker left searching becomes the next person to hide.

Murderer

Best suited for large groups. Roles are assigned secretly: one Sheriff and one Murderer. The Murderer "kills" players by winking at them discretely. When a player is winked at, they must announce their death after a short delay. The Sheriff's mission is to identify and catch the Murderer before everyone is eliminated.

The Village Sleeps

The classic "Werewolf" or "Mafia" game. The village is infiltrated by wolves who "kill" one villager every night. During the day, the survivors must debate and vote to execute a suspected wolf. Special roles like the Seer, the Doctor, or the Lovers add strategy and depth to the mystery.

The Conductor

Half of the group leaves the room. The remaining players stay and choose a "Director" and a specific "Action" (e.g., scratching their nose). Every time the Director performs the Action, the rest of the group must perform a second agreed-upon task (e.g., clapping once). The seekers return and must observe the group to identify who is the one triggering the group's reactions.

Logic & Riddles

Who enters the Ark?

A lateral thinking game where the leader says: "I'm going to the ark and I'm bringing..." followed by an object. Players must figure out the secret rule (e.g., only objects starting with your own name's first letter) to be allowed "on the ark".

If I were a...

Select one person in the group. Others take turns asking questions like "If they were a superhero, which one would they be?" or "If they were a brand of car...". Everyone answers, and then the person shares which comparisons felt most accurate.

Numbers

Assign a number to every player. 1 starts by calling another number (e.g., "1 to 5"). 5 must then call another (e.g., "5 to 3"). You lose if you hesitate, call your own number, or call a number not in play. Numbers are reassigned every round to keep players on their toes.

Contact

A game of mental agility and group telepathy. The objective is to force the "Guardian" to reveal letters of their secret word.

  • The Start: The Guardian thinks of a word (e.g., CASTLE) and says the first letter: "C".
  • The Challenge: Friends think of words starting with "C" and give a definition without saying the word: "It's a pet that meows".
  • The Contact: If someone else knows it (CAT), they shout "Contact!" and count "3, 2, 1...".
  • The Resolution: If they both say "CAT!" together, the Guardian must reveal the next letter ("A"). If the Guardian guesses it first ("No, it's not a CAT"), the letter remains hidden.

Icebreakers & Creativity

Two Truths and a Lie

Each person shares three statements about their life: two must be true, and one must be a lie. The group debates and votes on which one they think is the lie.

The Invisible Character

One person thinks of a celebrity. Others ask questions, but there's a catch: the questioner must act as if they are another famous character (e.g., asking like Messi or Batman). If the thinker can't guess who the questioner is imitating, they must provide an extra hint.

Fortunately / Unfortunately

A collaborative storytelling game. Players build a story phrase by phrase, always alternating between starting with "Fortunately..." and "Unfortunately...". (e.g., "I went to the beach." -> "Unfortunately, a shark appeared." -> "Fortunately, the shark was actually very friendly.")

Black Box

Also known as "Stories of Logic". The narrator describes a strange or paradoxical situation (e.g., "A man enters a bar, asks for a glass of water, the bartender points a shotgun at him, the man says thank you and leaves"). The group must solve the mystery by asking only "Yes" or "No" questions.
(Solution: The man had hiccups, and the scare cured him!)
