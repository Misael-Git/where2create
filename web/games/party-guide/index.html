Eeee Oooo

One person leaves the room while the group chooses a specific task for them to perform (e.g., "go to the kitchen for a glass, fill it with water and drink it" or "get on a chair and unscrew a light bulb"). When they return, they must guess and perform the task. As they move around, the group guides them: if they get closer to the objective, everyone says "Eeee"; if they move away, everyone says "Oooo". The person wins when they successfully complete the hidden task.

The Psychologist

One player acts as the "Psychologist" and leaves the room. The group decides on a collective "condition" or "illness" they all share (e.g., "everyone thinks they are a cat" or "everyone claps their hands whenever the Psychologist says a word starting with the letter A"). The Psychologist returns and must diagnose the condition by asking individual players questions. Players must answer only with "Yes" or "No". The game ends when the Psychologist correctly guesses the group's condition.

Impostor

A game of deception and quick thinking. Everyone in the group is given a secret word or context, except for one or two players (the Impostors). Everyone must take turns saying one word related to the secret. Impostors must try to blend in without knowing the word. After a round, everyone votes on who they think is the Impostor.

The Dark Room

A high-stakes variation of hide-and-seek played in a completely dark environment. One person hides, and the rest search for them. Seekers can call out to each other to confirm their locations, and others should reply to signal they are still searching. However, once a seeker finds the hidden person, they must silently hide with them and stop answering calls.

The game ends when only one seeker remains, or for a more interesting challenge, when the last person finally discovers the entire group. Even the smallest beam of light ruins the experience; therefore, the game must be played in total darkness, or ideally with seekers wearing blindfolds. The last seeker left searching becomes the next person to hide.

Challenging ones

Who enters the Ark?

A lateral thinking game where the leader says: "I'm going to the ark and I'm bringing..." followed by an object. Players must figure out the secret rule (e.g., only objects starting with your name's first letter or something the person to your right is wearing) to be allowed "on the ark".

The Prophet

One player leaves the room while the rest select a "secret person" from the group. When the player returns, they ask everyone: "If they were [a category], what would they be?". For example: "If they were a superhero, which one would they be?" or "If they were a brand of car...". After hearing the answers, the player must guess who the secret person is.

Numbers

Assign a number to every player. 1 starts by calling another number (e.g., "1 to 5"). 5 must then call another (e.g., "5 to 3"). You lose if you hesitate, call your own number, or call a number not in play. Numbers are reassigned every round to keep players on their toes.

Contact

A game of mental agility and group telepathy. The objective is to force the "Guardian" to reveal letters of their secret word.

  • The Start: The Guardian thinks of a word (e.g., CASTLE) and says the first letter: "C".
  • The Challenge: Friends think of words starting with "C" and give a definition without saying the word: "It's a pet that meows".
  • The Contact: If someone else knows it (CAT), they shout "Contact!" and count "3, 2, 1...".
  • The Resolution: If they both say "CAT!" together, the Guardian must reveal the next letter ("A"). If the Guardian guesses it first ("No, it's not a CAT"), the letter remains hidden.

Stories

Two Truths and a Lie

Each person shares three statements about their life: two must be true, and one must be a lie. The group debates and votes on which one they think is the lie.

Black Box

The narrator describes a strange or paradoxical situation (e.g., "A man enters a bar, asks for a glass of water, the bartender points a shotgun at him, the man says thank you and leaves"). The group must solve the mystery by asking only "Yes" or "No" questions.
(Solution: The man had hiccups, and the scare cured him!)

Fortunately / Unfortunately

A collaborative storytelling game. Players build a story phrase by phrase, always alternating between starting with "Fortunately..." and "Unfortunately...". (e.g., "I went to the beach." -> "Unfortunately, a shark appeared." -> "Fortunately, the shark was actually very friendly.")

Other ones

Murderer

Best suited for large groups. Roles are assigned secretly: one Sheriff and one Murderer. The Murderer "kills" players by winking at them discretely. When a player is winked at, they must announce their death after a short delay. The Sheriff's mission is to identify and catch the Murderer before everyone is eliminated.

The Village Sleeps

The classic "Werewolf" or "Mafia" game. The village is infiltrated by wolves who "kill" one villager every night. During the day, the survivors must debate and vote to execute a suspected wolf. Special roles like the Doctor or the Lovers add strategy and depth to the mystery.

The Conductor

Half of the group leaves the room. The remaining players stay and choose a "Director" from the other team and a specific "Action" (e.g., scratching their nose). Every time the Director performs the Action, the rest of the group must perform a second agreed-upon task (e.g., clapping once). The seekers return and must observe the group to identify who is the one triggering the group's reactions.
